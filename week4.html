Welcome & Integration Week

Welcome to Week 4. This is Integration Week—which means there's no new concept to absorb. This week is about consolidation.

You've spent three weeks building new muscle: the learner's posture, thinking in drafts, learning out loud. You've done experiments. You've reflected with Eko. You've felt the discomfort of doing something different. Now it's time to step back and ask: what's actually sticking? What shifted? And what do you want to deepen?

This isn't a victory lap. It's not a rest week. It's a different kind of work—the work of integration. You're going to review your experiments from Weeks 1-3, identify which practice had the most impact, and design a next-level version to try this week. Not a completely new experiment. The same practice, just one degree harder.

Why? Because learning doesn't happen in a single exposure. It happens through repetition with variation. You practiced the learner's posture once in Week 1. That planted the seed. This week, you're watering it.

Your goal this week: Reflect on Weeks 1-3, identify your highest-impact practice, and design a next-level experiment that deepens that muscle.

Part 2: Your Next-Level Experiment

A next-level experiment means you try the same thing again, but push yourself a bit further this time. Here's what that might look like:

🧠 If Week 1 Resonated (Learner's Posture)

Original: You asked AI to challenge your thinking on a safe topic.

Next level: Try it on something with real stakes. A decision you're actually weighing up. A plan you're about to commit to.

  • Have a live conversation with a colleague where you adopt the learner's posture
🔁 If Week 2 Resonated (Thinking in Drafts)

Original: You refined a prompt across three rounds on a straightforward task.

Next level: Try it on something bigger or more complex. A proposal, a safety report, a project plan.

  • Do 5 rounds instead of 3. See how far you can push it.
📢 If Week 3 Resonated (Learning Out Loud)

Original: You shared a messy moment with one person.

Next-level options:

  • Share with a larger group (your team, a Slack channel)
  • Share with someone more senior or in a more visible context
  • If you shared with Eko last time, share with an actual human this time
The rule: It should feel like a stretch, but not overwhelming. If it feels easy, go one degree harder. If it feels terrifying, scale it back.
